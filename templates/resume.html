How to write a good resume?

A resume is a formal document that outlines your professional background, including your work experience, education, skills, and achievements.
It is typically used when applying for a job to showcase your qualifications and convince potential employers that you are the right fit for the position.
A well-crafted resume helps highlight your strengths and makes a strong first impression, increasing your chances of getting an interview.

The connection between personal qualities and career success extends beyond just skills and abilities—it also impacts the workplace environment and relationships. A person with strong emotional intelligence, for example, is more likely to build meaningful professional connections, resolve conflicts effectively, and contribute to a positive team dynamic. Similarly, a proactive and ambitious mindset can lead to seizing new opportunities, driving innovation, and achieving long-term career growth.

Furthermore, the alignment between personal values and the type of work pursued is crucial. When a person's qualities resonate with their chosen field, they are more likely to find fulfillment and motivation, which enhances productivity and satisfaction. On the other hand, a mismatch between one’s traits and job requirements can lead to dissatisfaction, burnout, or stagnation.

What Documentation Should Be Prepared for Submission to an Employer?

To be hired, according to the current labor legislation in our country, the following documents must be presented:

1-Identity Documents

The primary document required for employment, in accordance with the Labor Code of Ukraine (KZOT), is a passport. It must be valid (provided in proper condition, with updated and certified photographs at the ages of 25 and 45, etc.). According to current legislation, individuals with a passport are to present only this document to their employer. In certain cases, other identity documents may be acceptable when hiring an employee. Employment is prohibited under the KZOT if the necessary identity documents are not provided at the time of hiring.

2-Educational Documents

Proof of qualifications and education is required in cases outlined by current regulatory legal acts. This may include a diploma of specialized education or a certificate of qualification. Qualification requirements for specific professions are determined by executive authorities.

For positions in regulated professions, such as engineering, medicine, or law, additional certification or licensure may be required to practice legally. Employers may also request evidence of participation in training programs, workshops, or professional development courses that align with the job's responsibilities.

3-Health Certificates

When applying for a job, a medical certificate confirming a medical examination, including laboratory and diagnostic tests, is required, along with any permissions established by law. This is mandatory for workers whose activities will involve difficult or particularly hazardous conditions.

The medical examination aims to ensure the applicant is fit for the job and does not pose a risk to themselves or others in the workplace. Additional health checks may be required periodically for employees working in high-risk environments, such as construction sites, factories, or roles involving exposure to harmful substances.

Specific industries, such as food production, healthcare, and childcare, often require more detailed health assessments, including tests for infectious diseases, mental health evaluations, and confirmation of vaccination records. Employers may request these certificates in compliance with sector-specific regulations to maintain workplace safety and public health standards.

4-Employment Record Book

Workers are not allowed to be hired without providing their employment record book. In cases where employment is the first in a person's life, only a passport and diploma are required. When entering into a labor contract, this document is essential. The employment record book must comply with legal requirements and contain complete information about the worker's employment history.

In addition, the HR department may require the following when hiring: an income declaration, a certificate of no criminal record or its expungement, proof of residence registration, referrals from educational institutions or employment centers, written references from previous employers, children’s birth certificates, lists of published works for researchers, a certificate for women changing their last name upon marriage, and, in the case of disability, a medical-social expert commission (MSEC) report, among others.
